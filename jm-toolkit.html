An LLM is a frozen state: trained on massive data and locked in place. The model itself doesn't change. But the products built on top of it (Copilot, ChatGPT) add layers that make it powerful: your prompt gives it context and instructions, tools let it search the web and create files, skills give it specialized know-how, and memory lets it remember your preferences over time. The LLM predicts the next word based on patterns. Everything else is scaffolding that makes that prediction remarkably useful.

How to Talk to AI
The single most important skill you'll develop.
Think of AI as a brilliant intern with ADHD.

Incredibly capable, but needs clear direction. Vague instructions produce vague results. Be specific about what you want, who you are, and what the output should look like. If you can write in English, you can do everything in this toolkit.

Be Specific
Don't say "check my emails." Say "what are the top 5 priority emails I need to respond to today, and draft a reply for each."
Give Context
Tell the AI who you are, who the audience is, and what you're trying to achieve. More context always equals better output. Set this up once in Custom Instructions.
Define the Output
Want a table? Say so. Want a Word document? Say so. Want a PowerPoint? Say so. Don't leave format to chance.
Iterate
First draft not right? Don't start over. Tell the AI what to fix. "Make it shorter," "add the rent roll data," "change the tone." Build on what you have.

Microsoft Copilot
Your new home screen for everything work-related. Microsoft's getting started guide →

Get Copilot Ready

You all have enterprise Copilot licenses. Complete these steps to unlock its full potential.

✓
Go to m365.cloud.microsoft and sign in with your work account. This is the enterprise version (look for the green checkmark confirming your data is secure)
✓
Click your profile (top right) and go to Settings > Personalization > Custom Instructions. Add your name, role, and communication style preferences. Learn about prompts →
✓
Learn the shortcut: Windows + C opens Copilot instantly over any window. Start building the muscle of calling AI before every task
✓
Download the Copilot mobile app (iOS / Android) for on-the-go access
Work vs. Web
The most important toggle. Work searches your internal data: emails, calendar, OneDrive/SharePoint files. Web searches the public internet. Use Work for internal tasks, Web for external research like market rates or regulations.
Mode Selection
Auto: default, uses multiple models. Quick Response: for simple tasks like proofreading. Think: for complex analysis like lease reviews. You can also select OpenAI direct under "More" for ChatGPT-quality responses inside Copilot.
Search Your Files
In the Work tab, use natural language to find files: "Find the lease for 127 West 26th Street" or "Show me the violation report from last month." Once files are on SharePoint, Copilot indexes everything automatically. (SharePoint migration is currently pending.)
Scheduled Prompts
Set up recurring prompts that run automatically. Example: every day at 6AM, get a summary of your top priority emails waiting for you when you arrive. Great for daily briefings, weekly lease expiry checks, or maintenance reminders.
Copilot in Your Apps

Copilot lives inside Outlook, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Teams. Look for the Copilot icon in each app's ribbon.

Outlook

Summarize email threads, draft replies, prioritize your inbox, and coordinate calendar slots with colleagues.

Word

Generate executive summaries, bank reports, and memos from your data. Drop in a spreadsheet and get a formatted document.

Excel

Use Agent Mode (under Tools) to build dashboards, create formulas, and analyze data in plain English.

PowerPoint

Generate presentations from spreadsheets or Word docs. Great for bank reports, investor updates, and team meetings.

Teams

Get meeting recaps, action items, and transcripts. Ask "What did I miss?" and Copilot summarizes the conversation.

Integrations

Connect Monday.com and other platforms to Copilot. Chat with your databases in natural language.

NotebookLM
Your personal research assistant that only knows what you give it. Google's official guide →

Get NotebookLM Ready

NotebookLM is a free Google tool. Upload documents and have AI conversations grounded only in those sources. No hallucinations from random web data. Google's guide

✓
Go to notebooklm.google.com and sign in with a Google account (personal is fine)
✓
Click New Notebook and upload sources: contracts, invoices, lease documents, inspection reports, or any PDF
✓
Download the NotebookLM app (iOS / Android) to listen to Audio Overviews on the go
Source-Grounded Chat
Ask questions and get answers only from your uploaded documents. Every answer cites the exact source. Upload all your elevator contracts and ask: "Are we being overcharged for any parts?"
Audio Overviews
One click turns your documents into a podcast-style audio summary. Great for wrapping your head around dense contracts, new regulations, or technical elevator repair invoices.
Data Tables
Extract structured data from contracts and invoices into clean tables. Compare terms across multiple vendor agreements. Export directly to spreadsheets.
Multi-Document Synthesis
Upload multiple contracts, maintenance records, or reports into one notebook. Ask questions across all of them: "Compare elevator costs across all buildings for the last 3 years."
Use case: Upload all elevator invoices, repair bills, and service contracts into one notebook. Then ask: "What parts are we paying the most for? Are any charges unusual compared to industry standards?" Every answer cites the exact invoice.

Workflows for Property Management
Copy-paste prompts built for the JM team's daily work.
Email Prioritization
Daily inbox triage, automated briefings
▾

Start every day with a clear picture of what needs your attention. Here's how to set up a scheduled prompt that runs automatically.

Sample Prompt (Work tab)
What are the top priority emails that need my attention today? Group them by urgency: time-sensitive coordination, tenant requests needing response, and internal items. For each, give me a one-line summary and who it's from.
How to schedule this:
1. Go to m365.cloud.microsoft and open Copilot chat
2. Type the prompt above in the Work tab
3. After you get a response, click the clock icon (Schedule) below the chat input
4. Choose "Every weekday" and set the time to 6:00 AM
5. Copilot will now send you this summary every morning before you arrive

You can create multiple scheduled prompts for different needs: a weekly lease expiry check, a monthly maintenance summary, etc. Browse more ideas at the Copilot Prompt Gallery.
Calendar Coordination
Find slots, schedule inspections, coordinate with teams
▾

Stop spending time cross-referencing calendars. Copilot reads everyone's calendar on the team and finds open slots.

Sample Prompt (Work tab)
Find me a 2-hour slot next week when both Eddie and I are free for a building inspection at 127 West 26th Street. Avoid Monday mornings. Create a calendar invite with the subject "Building Inspection - 127 W 26th" and include the address in the location.
Document Generation
Bank reports, executive summaries, and presentations from data
▾

Turn any spreadsheet into a professional Word document or PowerPoint in minutes. No more manually formatting bank reports.

Sample Prompt
I'm uploading our quarterly rent roll spreadsheet. Create a Word document with an executive summary that includes: total occupancy rate, revenue by building, lease expirations in the next 90 days, and any tenants with outstanding balances. Format it professionally for our bank's annual review.
Follow-up Prompt
Now create a PowerPoint presentation from this same data. Include charts for occupancy trends and revenue breakdown by building. Keep it to 8 slides max.
Bank reports: This used to take months of manual work. Now you upload the spreadsheet and Copilot generates the full report with formatting, charts, and narrative. Always double-check the numbers.
Excel Agent Mode
Dashboards, formulas, and data analysis in plain English
▾

In Excel, click Copilot > Tools > Agent Mode. This lets the AI take actions on your spreadsheet, not just answer questions about it.

Sample Prompt (Excel Agent Mode)
Build a dashboard tab to help me understand this data. Include: occupancy summary by building, a chart of monthly revenue trends, tenants with leases expiring in the next 6 months, and any units with below-market rent. Highlight anything that needs my attention.
Another Example
Find all tenants that are below market rent with leases expiring in the next 12 months. Create a new tab called "Renewal Targets" with their current rent, estimated market rate, and the potential revenue increase if renewed at market.
Invoice Processing & Yardi
Scan invoices, extract data, verify before entry
▾

Yardi integration with Copilot is not currently available out of the box and requires a custom configuration and setup process. In the meantime, you can still use Copilot to extract data from invoices and documents, then enter it into Yardi manually. This alone saves significant time.

Sample Prompt
I'm uploading a vendor invoice. Extract all line items including: vendor name, invoice number, date, description of work, quantity, unit price, and total. Format as a table. Flag any charges that seem unusually high for standard building maintenance work.
Note: Connecting Copilot directly to Yardi is possible but requires a dedicated setup engagement. Once configured, you would be able to say "Push this invoice data to Yardi" and it auto-populates the fields. For now, use Copilot to extract and verify data, then enter into Yardi manually.
